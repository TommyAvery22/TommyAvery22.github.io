interactive web project

MyoungAe Kim's portfolio home page

The initial thing I noticed about this website was its interactivity. Unlike many portfolios, this one was more engaging with its advent calendar providing feedback to the user. It feels like you are interacting with the site more than just merely viewing it. This playful feature piqued my curiosity and by capturing my interest immediately, I wanted to explore the site further despite its somewhat minimalist and uninteresting aesthetic design.

In the first two minutes of interacting with the web portfolio, my actions included:

  1. Scrolling down through the portfolio contents
  2. Clicking each of the boxes of the advent calender
  3. Dragging each of the advent contents around the page
  4. Clicking on the “Advent Calendar-like Web Portfolio” page
  5. Scrolling and viewing the individual project
  6. Clicking the remainder projects and scrolling through each of their contents

I spent the most time opening the advent calendar and dragging around its contents . Even though this calendar was a fairly simple novelty, it was an enjoyable little interactive experience that typically wouldn’t feature on a portfolio.

The most common action throughout these two minutes was clicking through the projects of the web portfolio and scrolling through each of their content. This repetitive action was due to the fact there were six projects featured.

Much like many portfolios, the primary goal here is to represent the individual's web design skills and expertise for the sake of employment. This is particularly showcased in the portfolio's layout, and shows the portfolio owner is capable of creating interactive experiences - particularly exemplified in the interactive door opening mechanic.

This goal has been communicated by the placement of the project showcase that takes centre stage from the portfolio's home page. The work's hierarchical importance is highlighted by the site's layout as the projects take up about 3/4 of the screens width. The portfolio showcases all projects from the home page which communicates that the individual is confident in all of their abilities. Contact details on the site's homepage - which includes LinkedIn, email, and Instagram - encourages engagement and further reinforces the site's goal of acting as means to create employment opportunities.

Over time, this portfolio site will mostly be interacted with during an employment recruitment process to reference or confirm skills and expertise. There may be no need for an employer to return on multiple occasions however, since the site is a portfolio, it is expected that the project page will be expanded on. A returning user would immediately just view any new projects that may have been added.

The interactive experience communicates its possible interaction over time by showcasing projects in a list-style format, combined with the contact information, it is implied that the site will be a resource for long-term professional engagement.

The aesthetic style of the site is very minimal and looks to reference a physical portfolio magazine through the use of its layout proportions, font sizes, minimal colour, and dividing lines. The minimalist aesthetic helps the projects stand out. The clean and structured layout reflects those typically found in high-quality print media. The font sizes remain readable while staying small enough to not take away from the images. Dividing lines are used to form sections, ensuring that the content is both visually appealing and easy to navigate. You are able to easily find projects because of this organisation. These design elements work together to give the digital experience a professional quality much like a physical professional portfolio.

This reference aims to emphasise the professionalism of the projects and that they should be analysed with the same amount of attentiveness that you would have when flipping through a physical portfolio.

The aesthetic style tells the viewer that they should feel confident in the work they are seeing and that it was completed to a professional standard. If an employer is viewing this, you would want them to feel as if the individual is confident in their work which in turn, makes the employer feel confident.

The most frustrating part of this interaction is the missed opportunity of really utilising the advent calendar. The curious-nature of playing with the calendar could have been taken advantage of by opening up to links for other pages within the site. It doesn’t really serve a purpose other than providing a satisfying interactive element for the user. The site itself is already quite shallow as there are really only two pages: the home page, and work showcase. Since there are only six projects showcased, the page can easily be fully explored in a matter of minutes. Separating the portfolio into more pages (e.g. the contact information could have a separate section, or be included in an ‘about me’ page) will give off the illusion that there is more depth and it won't feel as if there are not enough projects being featured.

The layout in project pages is aesthetically pleasing. The side panel containing the text and other projects remains fixed while allowing you to scroll through the images showcased on the right. This layout utilises the rule of thirds, and proudly shows off the project work by making it the main centrepiece of the page. Clicking through and viewing all of the projects is simple and doesn't feel disjointed. For an employer, it is important for them to be able to easily view projects quickly and the design of this page's interactive elements took this into consideration.
